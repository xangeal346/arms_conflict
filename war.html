What is it like to be patriotic?

One often assumes that being patriotic is engaging in the clandestine affairs that our government conducts without questions because it is believed that in doing so, one is making it easier for the leaders of our country to get rid of the threats harming the nation. As a result, the contriving members of our society think that their actions will renew vitality to our democracy. However, in engaging in such affairs, they cause the nation to repeat their mistakes, and in the process cause a loss of casualties. To be patriotic is to question what you are told, it’s to fight for the same inalienable basic rights as an individual, and for the adoption of ideas that will improve our relationships with others. It is what will uphold the true ideal of a democracy.

Why do people go to war?

The average American goes to war believing that they are defending their country from weapons of mass destruction, not knowing that their own country could have provoked a war by laying siege to the region of interest. The American government tends not to admit to their actual motive for involving their troops in several conflicts. It is because it’s in their interest. The government wishes to protect their businesses from rebels or invaders who have intentions of disrupting their flow of money, in which they make a profit from. The government does not want to be portrayed as indignant by the American public and so they keep the actual intentions a secret. As a person willing to sacrifice everything for liberty and peace, it would only be fair to know exactly what is being fought for.
